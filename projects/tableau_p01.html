IBM Employee Attrition Dashboard

Interactive Tableau dashboard and story demonstrating core employee demographics and attrition rates. It visualizes high-risk groups based on age, job satisfaction, education, and department to identify potential retention issues.

Data source: IBM HR Analytics Attrition Dataset (Kaggle)

← Back to Portfolio

What I Did

  • Extracted and processed the raw HR data to align with project goals.
  • Created essential Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) including Employee Count, Attrition Count, Attrition Rate (16.12%), and Avg. Age (37).
  • Developed calculated fields to bin employees into Age Groups and categorize Job Satisfaction ratings.
  • Designed a comprehensive dashboard featuring:
    • Attrition by Department Pie Chart.
    • Job Satisfaction by Job Role Heatmap.
    • Employee Age Distribution Histogram.
    • Attrition by Education Field Bar Chart.
    • Attrition by Gender and Age Group Donut Charts.
  • Organized the visuals to create a cohesive data story focused on identifying high-risk employee segments.

Key Insights

  • Department Attrition: Research & Development (R&D) accounts for 56.32% of the total churn (133 employees), showing the highest volume of attrition.
  • Age-Based Attrition: The 25-30 and 30-35 age groups represent the largest employee populations, making them critical focus areas for retention strategies.
  • Job Satisfaction Impact: Roles such as Laboratory Technicians and Sales Executives exhibit a high volume of low job satisfaction ratings (1 and 2), correlating with elevated attrition risk.
  • Gender and Age: The 30-35 Male age group has a particularly high attrition count (112), representing a significant segment of the total employee turnover.
